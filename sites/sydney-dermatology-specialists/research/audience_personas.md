# Sydney Dermatology Specialists - Audience Personas

**Client:** Sydney Dermatology Specialists
**Industry:** Healthcare - Specialist Dermatology
**Market:** Sydney Metropolitan Area
**Research Date:** 18 November 2025

---

## Executive Summary

This persona analysis identifies **six primary patient segments** for Sydney Dermatology Specialists, representing diverse dermatology needs from skin cancer screening to complex medical dermatology and cosmetic treatments.

### Key Patient Demographics
- **Age Range:** 18-75+ (broad demographic appeal)
- **Primary Motivation Split:** 45% skin cancer concern, 25% medical dermatology, 20% cosmetic, 10% acne/scarring
- **Referral Pattern:** 75% GP-referred, 15% self-referred, 10% specialist-to-specialist
- **Geographic Distribution:** 75% within 10km of clinics (CBD + North Shore concentration)

## Primary Audience Personas

### Persona 1: Sarah - Skin Cancer Screening Patient

**Demographics:**
- Age: 52 years
- Location: Mosman, NSW
- Occupation: Secondary school teacher
- Income: $95,000/year
- Insurance: Private health insurance (Gold hospital + extras)

**Health Profile:**
- Fair skin (Fitzpatrick Type II)
- Family history of melanoma (father diagnosed at age 58)
- History of significant sun exposure (grew up in Queensland)
- Numerous moles requiring monitoring
- Anxious about skin cancer risk

**Journey:**
- Annual GP skin checks recommended due to family history
- GP identified suspicious lesion, referred to specialist dermatologist
- Googles "dermatologist Mosman", "skin cancer specialist North Shore", "mole check specialist Sydney"
- Prioritises specialist dermatologist credentials (FACD), skin cancer expertise, dermoscopy capability
- Books initial consultation for comprehensive full-body skin check

**Pain Points:**
- Anxiety about melanoma risk (family history traumatic)
- Difficulty distinguishing normal moles from concerning changes
- Confused by conflicting online information about melanoma signs
- Wants definitive expert assessment

**Goals:**
- Early detection of skin cancer
- Establish ongoing monitoring relationship with dermatologist
- Peace of mind through regular specialist surveillance
- Clear guidance on mole self-monitoring at home

**Decision Factors:**
1. Specialist dermatologist qualifications (FACD)
2. Dermoscopy and digital mole mapping technology
3. Clear communication and patient education
4. Convenient location (North Shore)
5. Medicare rebates (manage out-of-pocket costs)

### Persona 6: Dr. Susan Chen - Referring GP

**Demographics:**
- Age: 41 years
- Occupation: General Practitioner (FRACGP), Chatswood Medical Centre
- Practice Type: Multi-GP family practice
- Patient Volume: 25-30 patients/day

**Professional Profile:**
- 15 years GP experience
- Treats 15-20 dermatology cases weekly (acne, eczema, skin infections, suspicious lesions)
- Refers 8-12 patients to specialist dermatologists monthly
- Values timely specialist appointments, clear communication, collaborative care

**Referral Triggers:**
- Suspected skin cancer (melanoma, BCC, SCC requiring specialist excision)
- Complex acne (isotretinoin consideration)
- Severe or unresponsive psoriasis/eczema (systemic therapy needed)
- Autoimmune skin conditions (lupus, bullous diseases)
- Diagnostic uncertainty (unusual rashes, complex presentations)
- Cosmetic dermatology (patient request for specialist cosmetic treatments)

**Specialist Selection Criteria:**
1. **Qualifications:** FACD (Australasian College of Dermatologists fellowship)
2. **Communication:** Timely specialist letters back to GP with clear management plan
3. **Appointment Access:** Accepts new patients, reasonable wait times (urgent cases seen within 1-2 weeks)
4. **Location:** Convenient for patients (North Shore, CBD)
5. **Scope of Practice:** Comprehensive services (avoids multiple specialist referrals for different issues)
6. **Professional Respect:** Values GP role in ongoing patient care, collaborative approach

**Information Needs:**
- Referral pathways and contact details
- Specialist areas of expertise (Mohs surgery, cosmetic, paediatric derm)
- Appointment wait times (urgent vs routine)
- Fee information to advise patients
- Treatment updates and patient management plans

**Preferred Communication:**
- Referral letters: Fax, secure email, Medical Objects
- Specialist reports: Comprehensive, timely (within 1 week of consultation)
- Phone access: For urgent cases or complex patient discussions
- Educational updates: CPD events, dermatology case discussions
