## 12. AHPRA Compliance for Medical Imagery

### 12.1 Dermatology-Specific Guidelines

**Allowed:**
- Professional medical consultations (generic, educational)
- Medical technology and equipment
- Facility and environment photography
- Specialist portraits with credentials
- Educational procedure imagery (non-graphic)
- Generic skin health concepts (abstract, non-identifying)

**Prohibited/Restricted:**
- Before/after cosmetic treatment images (unless with extensive disclaimers and patient consent)
- Identifiable patient images without written consent and release
- Graphic surgical or medical procedure images
- Implied guaranteed outcomes
- Testimonials or patient reviews (AHPRA restriction)
- Comparative claims (e.g., "better than other dermatologists")

**Cosmetic Dermatology Specific:**
- Cannot show dramatic before/after transformations
- Cannot create unrealistic expectations
- Must be evidence-based in claims
- Focus on medical expertise and safety, not vanity

---

### 12.2 Patient Imagery Consent

**If Using Real Patient Images:**
- Written informed consent required
- Model release forms signed
- Patients must understand image use (website, marketing)
- Right to withdraw consent
- De-identification where possible
- Prefer stock photography or AI-generated images to avoid consent complexity
