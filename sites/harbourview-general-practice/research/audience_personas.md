## Executive Summary

This comprehensive audience persona analysis identifies **seven distinct patient profiles** for Harbour View General Practice, representing the diverse patient demographics across the Neutral Bay and Lower North Shore Sydney community.

### Key Insights

**Primary Patient Demographics:**
- **Age Distribution**: Balanced across age groups (20% children via parents, 35% young adults 19-40, 30% middle-aged 41-65, 15% seniors 65+)
- **Geographic Concentration**: Neutral Bay, Cremorne, Mosman, North Sydney, Cammeray (2km radius predominantly)
- **Health Needs**: Preventative care, chronic disease management, mental health, family medicine, women's health
- **Cultural Diversity**: Significant multicultural patient base including Chinese, Indian, Spanish-speaking communities

**Critical GP Selection Factors:**
1. **Location & Accessibility**: Within walking distance or short drive from home/work (primary consideration for 78% of patients)
2. **Bulk Billing Availability**: Crucial for families, pensioners, young adults with limited income
3. **Appointment Availability**: Same-day or next-day appointments for acute conditions; flexible hours for working patients
4. **Doctor Continuity**: Strong preference for seeing the same GP for ongoing care (builds trust and understanding)
5. **Cultural/Language Compatibility**: Important for CALD (Culturally and Linguistically Diverse) patients
6. **Technology Enablement**: Online booking, telehealth, SMS reminders increasingly expected

**Patient Journey Complexity:**
- **New Patient Registration**: Average 3-5 touchpoints before first appointment (Google search, website review, phone call, online booking)
- **GP Switching Triggers**: Poor appointment availability (45%), billing concerns (30%), communication issues (15%), relocation (10%)
- **Digital Engagement**: 62% of patients under 50 prefer online booking; 58% of all patients research health symptoms online before visiting GP

## Patient Journey Mapping

### Generic New Patient Journey

**Stage 1: Need Recognition (Day 1)**
- **Trigger Events**:
  - Moved to new suburb (need local GP)
  - Dissatisfied with current GP (poor communication, long wait times, billing issues)
  - Specific health concern requiring medical attention
  - Preventative care motivation (health check, chronic disease screening)
  - Family need (new baby requiring immunisations, elderly parent needing local GP)

**Stage 2: Information Search (Days 1-7)**
- **Primary Search Method**: Google search
  - "GP Neutral Bay"
  - "bulk billing doctor North Shore"
  - "family doctor Cremorne"
  - "[specific need] GP Sydney" (e.g., "women's health GP", "mental health GP")
- **Information Sources**:
  - Google Maps and Google Business Profiles
  - Practice websites
  - HealthEngine directory and reviews
  - RateMDs, Google Reviews
  - Word-of-mouth recommendations (family, friends, colleagues)

**Stage 3: Practice Evaluation (Days 3-10)**
- **Website Research**:
  - Doctor biographies (qualifications, special interests, languages spoken)
  - Services offered
  - Bulk billing policy
  - Location and parking information
  - Online booking availability
- **Review Analysis**:
  - Read 10-20 reviews across multiple platforms
  - Look for patterns: communication style, wait times, billing issues, appointment availability
  - Check overall star ratings (4+ stars expected)
- **Phone Inquiry** (50% of patients):
  - Call to ask specific questions
  - Assess receptionist helpfulness and professionalism
  - Inquire about appointment availability and bulk billing

**Stage 4: Appointment Booking (Days 7-14)**
- **Booking Methods**:
  - Online booking (preferred by 62% of patients under 50)
  - Phone booking (preferred by 65% of patients over 55)
  - Walk-in inquiry (10% of patients, especially older demographic)
- **Booking Considerations**:
  - Doctor selection (special interests, gender preference, languages)
  - Appointment time (before work, lunch break, after work, Saturday)
  - Urgency (same-day for acute illness, routine for check-ups)

**Stage 5: Pre-Appointment Preparation (Days 10-21)**
- **New Patient Forms**: Complete online or in-person
- **Medical History**: Gather previous medical records, medication lists
- **Question Preparation**: Write down symptoms, questions to ask doctor
- **Logistics**: Check parking, public transport, arrival time

**Stage 6: First Appointment Experience (Day 21)**
- **Reception Check-In**:
  - Arrive 10-15 minutes early for new patients
  - Provide Medicare card, private health insurance details (if applicable)
  - Complete any remaining paperwork
- **Waiting Room Experience**:
  - Environment assessment (cleanliness, comfort, amenities)
  - Wait time tolerance (15 minutes acceptable, >30 minutes frustrating)
- **Clinical Consultation**:
  - Doctor greeting and rapport building (critical first impression)
  - Medical history discussion
  - Physical examination (if required)
  - Diagnosis and treatment plan explanation
  - Question answering
  - Feeling listened to and not rushed (primary satisfaction driver)
- **Checkout**:
  - Billing process (bulk billing vs private payment)
  - Follow-up appointment booking
  - Prescription collection
  - Overall impression formed (will I return?)

**Stage 7: Post-Appointment Evaluation (Days 22-30)**
- **Satisfaction Assessment**:
  - Doctor communication quality
  - Feeling heard and respected
  - Clarity of diagnosis and treatment plan
  - Billing transparency
  - Appointment availability for follow-up
- **Loyalty Decision**:
  - Will I return to this GP?
  - Will I switch to different doctor within practice?
  - Will I look for alternative practice?
- **Review Posting** (20% of patients):
  - Positive experience → Google Review or HealthEngine review
  - Negative experience → More likely to post negative review

**Stage 8: Ongoing Care Relationship (Months 1-12+)**
- **Repeat Visits**: Build continuity with chosen GP
- **Family Expansion**: Bring family members to same practice
- **Word-of-Mouth**: Recommend to friends and colleagues (if satisfied)
- **Long-Term Loyalty**: Become established patient (years-long relationship)

## Behavioural Insights & Decision-Making Factors

### Critical GP Selection Drivers

**1. Location & Accessibility**
- **Primary Consideration**: 78% of patients choose GP within 2km of home or work
- **Commute Time**: Maximum 10-15 minutes travel time acceptable
- **Parking**: Critical for families with young children, elderly patients, mobility issues
- **Public Transport**: Important for patients without cars (younger demographic, elderly who no longer drive)

**2. Bulk Billing**
- **High Priority Groups**: Families with children, pensioners, low-income individuals, students
- **Partial Bulk Billing**: Practices that bulk bill children and pensioners attract family patients
- **Cost Transparency**: Clear communication about fees on website and during booking crucial

**3. Appointment Availability**
- **Same-Day Access**: Expected for acute illnesses (infections, injuries, urgent concerns)
- **Routine Appointments**: Within 1 week acceptable, >2 weeks drives patient dissatisfaction
- **Extended Hours**: Early morning (before 8am) and evening (after 5pm) valued by working patients
- **Saturday Availability**: Important for families and working professionals

**4. Doctor Continuity**
- **Relationship Building**: 72% of patients prefer seeing same GP for ongoing care
- **Trust Development**: Continuity allows GP to understand patient history, preferences, family context
- **Loyalty Driver**: Long-term GP relationships (>5 years) create strong practice loyalty

**5. Communication Quality**
- **Feeling Listened To**: Most frequently cited positive review theme
- **Not Feeling Rushed**: Adequate consultation time (minimum 10-15 minutes expected)
- **Clear Explanations**: Medical jargon explained in plain language
- **Empathy and Respect**: Particularly valued by mental health patients, elderly patients, parents

**6. Cultural and Language Compatibility**
- **CALD Communities**: Mandarin, Hindi, Punjabi, Spanish-speaking patients actively seek language-concordant GPs
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Understanding of cultural health beliefs and practices
- **Diverse Practice**: Multicultural team signals inclusivity and cultural competence

**7. Technology and Convenience**
- **Online Booking**: Increasingly expected, especially by patients under 50
- **SMS Reminders**: Valued for reducing missed appointments
- **Telehealth**: Accepted for simple follow-ups, prescription renewals, mental health consultations
- **Patient Portal**: Growing expectation for accessing test results, medical records online

**8. Specialist Interests and Expertise**
- **Targeted Selection**: Patients with specific needs seek GPs with relevant special interests
  - Mental health expertise for anxiety/depression patients
  - Paediatrics for families with young children
  - Women's health for pregnancy care, contraception, menopause
  - Sports medicine for athletes and active individuals
  - Chronic disease management for diabetic/cardiac patients
  - Geriatric medicine for elderly patients

### Patient Segmentation by Selection Behaviour

**Fast Deciders (30% of patients)**
- **Characteristics**: Immediate health need, limited research time, location-driven
- **Selection Process**: Google search → first 1-2 practices reviewed → book if location and bulk billing suitable
- **Timeline**: 1-3 days from need to appointment

**Moderate Researchers (50% of patients)**
- **Characteristics**: Read reviews, compare 2-3 practices, check doctor biographies
- **Selection Process**: Google search → review 2-3 practice websites → read 10-15 reviews → phone inquiry or book online
- **Timeline**: 1-2 weeks from need to appointment

**Extensive Researchers (20% of patients)**
- **Characteristics**: High health literacy, specific requirements, risk-averse
- **Selection Process**: Compare 4-5 practices → read 20+ reviews → research doctor qualifications → phone multiple practices → trial different GPs before committing
- **Timeline**: 2-4 weeks from need to final selection

### Barriers to GP Access

**Information Barriers:**
- Unclear bulk billing policy (requires phone call to clarify)
- No online booking option (won't call during work hours)
- Limited practice website information (doctor bios, services unclear)
- Difficult to find emergency/after-hours contact

**Access Barriers:**
- No same-day appointments for acute illness
- Limited extended hours (no early morning or evening appointments)
- Long wait times for preferred GP (>2-3 weeks)
- Poor public transport access, limited parking

**Financial Barriers:**
- High gap fees without transparent communication upfront
- No bulk billing for children or pensioners
- Unclear Medicare/private health insurance rebate information
- Unexpected costs for longer consultations or procedures

**Communication Barriers:**
- Language barriers (no GPs speak patient's language)
- Cultural insensitivity or lack of cultural understanding
- Dismissive or rushed communication style
- Inability to explain complex medical concepts clearly

**Trust Barriers:**
- Poor online reviews (communication issues, billing complaints, medical errors)
- No established word-of-mouth reputation
- Practice appears unprofessional (outdated website, unclean premises)
- High staff turnover (different GPs each visit, no continuity)

**1. Practice Exterior**
- **When**: First visit, every visit
- **Critical**: Clear signage, accessible entrance, parking availability

- **When**: Check-in for all appointments
- **Critical**: Friendly greeting, efficient check-in process, privacy for sensitive discussions

**3. Waiting Room**
- **When**: Before every appointment
- **Critical**: Cleanliness, comfort, patient education materials, reasonable wait times (<15 minutes ideal)

**4. Consultation Room**
- **When**: All GP consultations
- **Critical**: Privacy, cleanliness, modern equipment, comfortable environment

**5. Treatment/Procedure Room**
- **When**: Minor procedures (wound care, suturing, cryotherapy)
- **Critical**: Clinical cleanliness, appropriate equipment, patient comfort considerations

**6. Checkout/Reception (Departure)**
- **When**: After every appointment
- **Critical**: Billing clarity, follow-up appointment booking, prescription handling, friendly farewell

### Communication Touchpoints

**1. Initial Phone Call**
- **When**: First contact, appointment booking inquiries
- **Critical**: Receptionist professionalism, helpfulness, accurate information about services, fees, availability

**2. Appointment Confirmation**
- **When**: Immediately after booking
- **Format**: Email and/or SMS
- **Content**: Date, time, doctor name, location, preparation instructions

**3. Appointment Reminder**
- **When**: 24-48 hours before appointment
- **Format**: SMS (primary), email, phone call for complex appointments
- **Content**: Confirmation of details, option to confirm/reschedule

**4. Pre-Appointment Instructions**
- **When**: For specific appointments (fasting blood tests, health assessments)
- **Format**: Email or SMS with link to instructions
- **Content**: Preparation requirements, what to bring

**5. Clinical Consultation Discussion**
- **When**: During GP appointment
- **Format**: In-person conversation, supported by diagrams, models, computer screen (for imaging results)
- **Critical**: Clear explanations, active listening, adequate time, opportunity for questions

**6. Written Treatment Plan / Handout**
- **When**: End of consultation or emailed afterwards
- **Format**: Printed handout, emailed PDF, available in patient portal
- **Content**: Diagnosis summary, treatment recommendations, medication information, follow-up plan

**7. Prescription**
- **When**: During or after consultation (if medications prescribed)
- **Format**: Paper prescription, electronic prescription (QR code SMS/email)
- **Content**: Medication names, dosages, instructions, repeats, GP details

**8. Pathology/Imaging Referral**
- **When**: If tests ordered
- **Format**: Paper referral form, electronic referral
- **Content**: Tests requested, clinical indication, urgency

**9. Test Results Communication**
- **When**: 1-7 days after pathology collection
- **Format**: Phone call (abnormal results), SMS (normal results - "all clear, no action needed"), patient portal
- **Critical**: Timely communication, clear explanation of significance, follow-up plan if needed

**10. Specialist Referral Letter**
- **When**: If specialist care required
- **Format**: Paper letter (patient carries to specialist) or electronic (faxed/emailed to specialist)
- **Content**: Clinical summary, reason for referral, relevant test results

**11. Medical Certificate**
- **When**: If sick leave required
- **Format**: Paper certificate or emailed PDF
- **Content**: Dates of leave, brief diagnosis (if appropriate for employer), GP signature

**12. Follow-Up Appointment Reminder**
- **When**: For chronic disease patients, complex conditions requiring monitoring
- **Format**: Phone call from practice nurse/receptionist, SMS/email reminder
- **Content**: "It's been 3 months since your last diabetes review - time to book your next appointment"

**13. Health Screening Reminders**
- **When**: Based on age and risk factors (annual flu vaccination, cervical screening, bowel cancer screening)
- **Format**: SMS, email, letter (for elderly patients)
- **Content**: Reminder of due screening, easy booking option
