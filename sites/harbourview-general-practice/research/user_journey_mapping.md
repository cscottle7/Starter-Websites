# User Journey Mapping: Harbour View General Practice
## Comprehensive Patient Journey Analysis Across Life Stages and Health Needs

**Prepared:** November 2025
**Location:** Neutral Bay, Lower North Shore Sydney
**Service Lines:** Family Medicine, Chronic Disease Management, Mental Health, Women's Health, Children's Health

---

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive user journey mapping analyses patient experiences across five distinct patient personas for Harbour View General Practice. By mapping emotional states, information needs, pain points, and opportunities at each journey stage, this document provides strategic insights for optimising patient acquisition, satisfaction, retention, and advocacy.

### Key Journey Insights

**Journey Duration Variance:**
- New patient acquisition: 1-3 weeks (first-time patients)
- Urgent care pathway: Same day to 48 hours (acute illness)
- Chronic disease management: Lifelong relationship (years)
- Preventative health: Annual to biennial touchpoints
- Mental health care: 3-12 month intensive engagement

**Critical Conversion Points:**
1. Website first impression (78% of patients research online first)
2. Phone/online booking experience (62% abandon if complex)
3. First consultation quality (83% loyalty decision made here)
4. Billing transparency and value perception (30% switch due to cost concerns)
5. Appointment availability for follow-up (45% leave due to poor access)

---

## Journey Framework: Six Universal Stages

All patient journeys progress through six interconnected stages, though timeline and emotional intensity vary by patient segment:

1. **Awareness** - Recognising need for GP care
2. **Research** - Comparing GP options, gathering information
3. **Decision** - Selecting Harbour View GP and booking first appointment
4. **First Experience** - Initial consultation and first impressions
5. **Ongoing Care** - Building trust, continuity, relationship deepening
6. **Advocacy** - Recommending practice to family, friends, colleagues

## Cross-Journey Insights: Universal Themes

### What All Successful Patient Journeys Have in Common

#### 1. First Consultation Excellence
**Make-or-Break Moment:**
- 83% of patients decide whether to return based on first consultation
- Critical elements:
  - Feeling listened to (not rushed)
  - Empathetic communication
  - Clear explanations
  - Collaborative approach
  - Adequate time allocated

#### 2. Continuity of Care
**Loyalty Driver:**
- 72% of patients prefer seeing same GP
- Long-term relationships (5+ years) create unbreakable loyalty
- Continuity allows GP to truly know patient (history, context, preferences)

#### 3. Accessibility When It Matters
**Trust Test:**
- Same-day appointments for urgent needs
- Responsive to crises (sick babies, acute illnesses, health scares)
- Practices that fail accessibility test lose patients permanently

#### 4. Respect & Dignity
**Fundamental Need:**
- Patients want to feel valued, not processed
- Personalized greetings ("Good morning Emma, how have you been?")
- Remembering details from previous visits
- Treating patients as individuals, not numbers

#### 5. Clear Communication
**Reducing Anxiety:**
- Medical jargon explained in plain language
- Diagnosis and treatment plans clearly communicated
- Questions welcomed and answered thoroughly
- Follow-up instructions provided (verbal + written)

## Strategic Recommendations

### Priority 1: Optimize First Visit Experience
**Objective:** Ensure 90%+ first-visit patients return

**Tactics:**
1. Pre-appointment welcome email with preparation tips
2. Reception staff trained in warm, personalized greetings
3. Minimal wait times (<15 minutes)
4. Adequate consultation times (minimum 20 minutes for complex cases)
5. Post-appointment follow-up (SMS or email: "Thank you for visiting Harbour View GP")

---

### Priority 2: Enhance Continuity of Care
**Objective:** Increase same-GP booking rate to 85%+

**Tactics:**
1. Online booking defaults to "your usual doctor" for established patients
2. Receptionist training: "Would you like to book with Dr. Sharma again, or see another doctor?"
3. Appointment reminder SMS mentions doctor by name: "Appointment tomorrow with Dr. Chen"
4. Long-term appointment booking enabled (book 3-6 months ahead for chronic disease patients)

---

### Priority 3: Accessibility Excellence
**Objective:** Same-day appointments available for urgent cases, <1 week for routine

**Tactics:**
1. Reserve 20% of daily appointments for same-day urgent cases
2. Triage protocol: reception staff trained to identify urgent needs
3. Extended hours: early morning (7:30am) and evening (6:30pm) appointments 2x weekly
4. Saturday appointments (9am-1pm)
5. Telehealth for simple follow-ups (reduces pressure on in-person slots)

---

### Priority 4: Communication Excellence
**Objective:** 95%+ patient satisfaction with GP communication

**Tactics:**
1. GP training: patient-centered communication, empathy, active listening
2. Written summaries for complex diagnoses (patient takes home)
3. Post-consultation SMS: "If you have questions about today's appointment, please call us"
4. Test result communication within 7 days (phone for abnormal, SMS for normal)

---

### Priority 5: Digital Patient Experience
**Objective:** Seamless digital touchpoints supporting journey

**Tactics:**
1. Mobile-optimised online booking (3 steps maximum)
2. SMS appointment reminders (24 hours before)
3. Email confirmations with preparation instructions
4. Patient portal (access test results, request prescription renewals)
5. Telehealth platform for appropriate consultations
