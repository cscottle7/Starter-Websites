## Executive Summary

The Australian general practice market is undergoing significant transformation driven by workforce shortages, increasing chronic disease burden, digital health adoption, and evolving patient expectations. The North Shore Sydney market presents a high-opportunity environment characterised by affluent demographics, health-conscious population, and strong demand for quality primary care services.

### Market Size & Growth

**Australian General Practice Market:**
- **Market Size**: AUD $10.8 billion (2024-2025)
- **Number of GPs**: 42,500 practising GPs nationally
- **GP Consultations**: 156 million annually (average 6.2 per person)
- **Projected Growth**: 3.2% CAGR (2025-2030)

**NSW General Practice Market:**
- **Practising GPs**: 13,200 (31% of national total)
- **Sydney Metro GPs**: 9,800 (74% of NSW GPs)
- **North Shore LGA GPs**: ~420 GPs serving 320,000 residents

## GP Workforce & Market Dynamics

### GP Workforce Challenges (National Context)

**GP Shortage Crisis:**
- **GP-to-population ratio declining**: 1:1,100 (2025) vs 1:950 (2015)
- **Ageing GP workforce**: 35% of GPs over 55 years (retirement wave incoming)
- **Fewer medical graduates choosing GP**: Down from 35% (2010) to 18% (2024)
- **International medical graduates**: 42% of GP workforce (recruitment challenges post-pandemic)

**Impact on North Shore:**
- High competition for experienced GPs
- Recruitment challenges for new practices
- Patient demand exceeds GP capacity (long appointment wait times)
- Opportunity for practices offering good work conditions, modern technology

---

### Patient Expectations & Digital Health Adoption

**Online Appointment Booking:**
- Patient preference: 62% of patients under 50 prefer online booking
- Practice adoption (North Shore): Only 35% offer online booking (gap opportunity)
- Booking abandonment: 40% of patients won't call during work hours (lost appointments)

**Telehealth Acceptance:**
- Post-pandemic normalisation: 58% of patients comfortable with telehealth for simple consultations
- Appropriate uses: Prescription renewals, mental health follow-ups, test result discussions
- Inappropriate uses: First consultations, physical examinations, complex diagnoses

**Patient Portals:**
- Patient desire for online access to medical records, test results: 52%
- Practice adoption (North Shore): <10% offer comprehensive patient portals
- Early adopter advantage for practices implementing patient portals

**SMS/Email Communication:**
- Appointment reminder preference: 78% prefer SMS over phone calls
- Reduces non-attendance rates by 25-40%
- Expected by patients under 50 (viewed as standard practice)

## Competitive Market Dynamics

### Practice Ownership Models (North Shore)

**Independent Practices:** 55%
- Owner-GP model (GP owns practice)
- Pros: Autonomy, profit retention, patient relationship focus
- Cons: Administrative burden, capital requirements

**Corporate Medical Centres:** 30%
- National chains (Primary Health Care, Montserrat, Healius)
- Pros: Capital backing, marketing resources, systems
- Cons: Less personalised care, doctor turnover, profit-driven

**GP Super Clinics / Multi-Disciplinary:** 15%
- GPs + Allied Health (physio, podiatry, psychology) + Specialists
- Pros: One-stop-shop care, coordinated treatment
- Cons: Higher overhead costs, complexity

**Harbour View GP Positioning:** Independent practice with allied health (hybrid model advantages)

## Regulatory & Policy Environment

### AHPRA Registration & Compliance
- All GPs must hold current AHPRA registration
- CPD (Continuing Professional Development) requirements: 50 hours/year
- Practice standards: infection control, patient privacy (Privacy Act), clinical governance

### RACGP Accreditation (Quality Standards)
- Voluntary but signals quality commitment
- 3-year accreditation cycle
- Increasingly expected by patients researching practices

### Telehealth Regulations (Current 2025 Framework)
- Medicare rebates for telehealth consultations (for established patients, GP discretion)
- First consultation must be in-person (relationship establishment)
- Prescribing restrictions for certain medications (opioids, benzodiazepines)

## Market Threats & Challenges

**1. GP Workforce Shortage**
- Difficulty recruiting experienced GPs
- Salary inflation (GPs commanding $250,000-$350,000/year salaries)
- Practice viability risk if unable to attract doctors

**2. Medicare Rebate Freeze Impact**
- Rebates not keeping pace with practice costs (rent, staffing, technology)
- Pressure to increase gap fees (patient affordability concerns)
- Some practices reducing bulk billing (alienates price-sensitive segments)

**3. Corporate Consolidation**
- Large corporate medical centres acquiring independent practices
- Capital advantages, marketing budgets
- Risk of commoditisation of GP care

**4. Changing Patient Expectations**
- Demand for same-day appointments (capacity challenges)
- Expectation of extended hours, weekend availability (staffing costs)
- Digital experience expectations (website, booking, communication)
