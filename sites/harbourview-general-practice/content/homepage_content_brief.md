**Heading (H2):**

**Format:** Accordion or expandable Q&A

**Q1: Do you bulk bill?**
A: Yes, we bulk bill children under 16, pensioners, concession card holders, and certain eligible services such as mental health care plans and GP management plans. For other consultations, we charge private fees with Medicare rebates available.

**Q2: How do I book an appointment?**
A: You can book appointments online 24/7 through our website, or call us during opening hours on (02) 9953 XXXX. Same-day appointments are often available for urgent medical concerns.

**Q3: Can I see the same doctor each visit?**
A: Absolutely! We encourage continuity of care and recommend booking with the same GP for ongoing health needs. You can select your preferred doctor when booking online or request them when calling.

**Q4: Do you offer telehealth appointments?**
A: Yes, we offer telehealth consultations via video call for established patients. Telehealth is suitable for simple follow-ups, prescription renewals, mental health consultations, and test result discussions. Your first consultation must be in-person.

**Q5: What should I bring to my first appointment?**
A: Please bring your Medicare card, private health insurance details (if applicable), current medications list, and any recent test results or specialist letters. Arrive 10 minutes early to complete new patient registration forms.

**Q6: Do you have multilingual doctors?**
A: Yes! Dr. James Chen speaks Mandarin, Dr. Priya Sharma speaks Hindi and Punjabi, and Dr. Emily Rodriguez speaks Spanish. You can request a specific doctor when booking your appointment.

## Content Tone & Style Guidelines

**Voice:** Professional yet warm, approachable, empathetic
**Perspective:** First-person plural ("we", "our") to create welcoming, personal connection
**Language:** Clear, jargon-free (define medical terms when used), conversational
**Sentence Structure:** Short to medium sentences, active voice, scannable paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)

**Do:**
- Use inclusive language ("families", "individuals", "patients of all ages")
- Emphasise patient-centred care and empathy
- Highlight convenience and accessibility
- Be transparent about fees and billing
- Use Australian spelling (centre, practise, recognise)

**Don't:**
- Use overly clinical or technical medical jargon
- Make health claims that can't be substantiated
- Use stock photos (patients can tell - use real practice photos if possible)
- Create walls of text (use headings, bullet points, white space)
- Oversell or use hyperbolic marketing language
